Introduction

Human curiosity to discover and explore the unknown equally drives our attempts to expand our knowledge and understand the universe (Eicher, 2005). Thus, humankind's progression into space was a collaborative effort supported by intrepid pioneers such as visionaries, scientists, and scholars. It was those types of pioneers and futurists that excited people's imaginations about traveling to the stars. Then during the cold war era, an international space race started between Russia and America competing to be the first to put a person into space. Russia became first to put a man in orbit; however, America was first to land on the Moon. In light of NASA's success, the organization decided to schedule numerous expeditions to space marking a new era for humankind (Price, 1996). The exploration of space benefits humanity through advancements in technological innovations, the need to colonize space and the cross-cultural impact on society.

The Human Benefits of Space-Based Technology

The human benefits of space-based technology have left its footprint on Earth as well as the solar system, from satellites and telerobotic space probes to composite golf clubs and plastic bags. Moreover, humanity's exploration of space has, as a result, advanced the development of technological innovations that people use every day, such as, cellular phones, digital video DivX players, smoke detectors, and Global Positioning System (GPS) Navigators (Eicher, 2005). Furthermore, the International Space Station (ISS) and its crew carry out Earth observation and disaster assistance. The ISS crew obtains data from onboard sensors and imagery and Earth observation satellites to provide accurate atmospheric, geological and meteorological information of affected regions (NASA; NASA, International, 2013). As a result, the technology developed from the space program has changed and revolutionized the way people communicate, navigate, manage and maintain their lives.

The Human Benefits of Space Colonization

Space colonization is advantageous to humanity and its survival in the wake of a potential global catastrophe and at the same time expanding humankind's capabilities. Though Earth is in no imminent global threat, numerous occurrences in space, such as a meteorite or a massive sun flare could wipe out all life. Therefore, as a species, we should commence with the colonizing of space and discover a hospitable planet for a relocation point. In effect, those efforts will help to develop alternative means of interstellar space travel and provide the critical knowledge needed for Earth's expansion into space (Johnson, 2012).

Stephen Hawking stated, "I do not think the human race will survive the next thousand years unless we spread into space. There are too many accidents that can befall life on a single planet" (The EU and space, 2009, para. 8).

Overall, colonizing the solar system provides human-based interests that will help in solving fundamental scientific questions concerning the formation of the solar system as well as contribute to developing humanities continued space exploration and colonization efforts.

The Human Benefits of Space exploration On Our Culture

Space exploration benefits humanity by its cross-cultural impact on society through musical compositions, science fiction cinemas, space camps, and UFO research. Several musicians have used space travel as subject matter in the creation of lyrics for songs, and videos such as Pink Floyd's Dark Side of the Moon or David Bowie's Space Oddity. Moreover, science fiction evolved into a pop-culture all of its own as a result of innovative features used in films like Frank Herbert's Dune, or Gene Roddenberry's Star Trek (McLeod, 2003). Plus, if someone wanted a chance to feel like an astronaut, he or she could attend a space camp to experience different aspects of the astronauts training. On the other hand, there is a little city in New Mexico named Roswell where UFO incidents have occurred. Also located there is a UFO Research Center for the cataloging, archiving, and study of reported close encounters (Explore space from earth, 2013). Conclusion

Human research expeditions to space have benefited our efforts towards expanding our diverse understanding of science and fellowship in many areas. Since the space program started, a plethora of technological innovations was discovered and can be noticed almost anyplace. Also, yet most people do not know about them. By placing colonies in space, we can improve the continuation of human civilization through exploration and better understand cosmic phenomena. When a person thinks of "Flash Gordon" or how far back science fiction stretches, as a whole, the cultural influence can be seen on a generational level that has touched countless people. The exploration of space has yielded astonishing feats and also improved people's lives because of the effort of pioneers and visionaries who saw space the way we see it now.
